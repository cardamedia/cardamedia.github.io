StartUp Marketing Method

Stage 1 - Feasibility Study

01

Value Proposition

A clear and concise value proposition is essential for any startup that wants to succeed. It should clearly state the benefits of your products or services, and why customers should choose you over your competitors.

02

Competitive Analysis

Conducting a competitive analysis to learn about your competitors' strengths, weaknesses, and strategies will help to develop a winning marketing plan that will boost your business growth.

03

Segmentation

The process of dividing your target market into smaller groups based on shared characteristics. This allows the creation of more relevant and effective marketing campaigns which resonate with your audience.

04

Business Model

A business model helps to identify target markets, create a value proposition, and develop a sustainable revenue stream.

05

Breakthrough Innovation

Breakthrough innovation is a new product, service, or process that significantly changes the way we live and work. It can be disruptive, challenging the status quo and creating new markets

06

Customer Journey Map

A Customer Journey Map is a visual representation of the experiences a customer has with your company, from first contact to purchase to post-purchase. It helps you to understand your customers' needs and pain points, and identify opportunities to improve the customer experience.

07

Financial Assessment

The process to determine an your organization's financial situation, typically for the purpose of making a decision about the development of products or services

Stage 2 - Hypothesis and Experiments

08

Hypothesis Verification

Hypothesis verification is the process of using statistical methods to determine whether there is enough evidence to support a hypothesis. The four steps of hypothesis verification are: (1) state the hypotheses, (2) set the criteria for a decision, (3) compute the test statistic, and (4) make a decision.

09

Minimal Viable Product Types

Minimal Viable Product (MVP) is a product with just enough features to satisfy early customers and validate a product idea.

10

Validate Problem-Solution-Market Fit

Problem-Solution-Market Fit is the process of ensuring that your product solves a real problem for a large enough group of people who are willing to pay for it. It is the first step towards building your successful business.

11

Positioning Statement

A positioning statement is a brief description of a product or service that explains how it fulfills a particular need of the target market. It is used to guide marketing and sales efforts and to differentiate the product or service from the competition.

12

Communication & Distribution Channels

Communication & Distribution Channels are the ways that businesses reach their customers and get their products or services into their hands. The right channels will vary depending on the business and its target market.

13

Profit & Growth Potential

Profit and growth potential refers to the ability of your company to generate profits and increase its revenue over time. This is important for investors, as it indicates that your company is healthy and has the potential to generate returns.

14

Alternative Marketing Strategies

lternative marketing strategies are unconventional ways to reach your target audience and promote your brand. They can be more effective than traditional marketing methods, but they often require more creativity and effort.

Stage 3 - Fundraising

15

Fundraising Process

A systematic approach to identifying, cultivating, and soliciting capital from individuals, organizations, and foundations.

16

Fundraising Milestones

Break down your fundraising goal into smaller, achievable goals

17

Bootstrapping & Plan B

Bootstrapping is a process of starting a business or project with little to no outside investment. It involves using your own resources, such as savings, personal time, and sweat equity, to get the venture off the ground.

18

Best Funding Source

Funds can come from different sources - they can be a grant, bond, federal or state award, private donation, or internal money that is allocated for your company.

19

Fundraising Material

Fundraising materials are different types of marketing material for the specific purpose to raise funding for your company.

20

Startup Valuation

Startup valuation is the process of determining the worth of your company. It is often based on factors such as revenue, growth potential, and intellectual property.

21

Target Actions

Target Actions are a activities to achieve a specific goal, e.g. to raise the required funds for your company

Stage 4 - Product Launch

22

The Myth of Big Bang

To launch in a Big Bang approach is often overrated and does not lead to the expected results. Alternative ways to launch your product or service can be more successful.

23

Brand Building

Brand building is the process of creating a strong and positive perception of your product or service in the minds your consumers. It involves developing a unique brand identity, positioning the brand in the marketplace, and communicating the brand message effectively.

24

Customer Touchpoints

A customer touchpoint is the place where interaction with your customer takes place. Understanding these touchpoints, you can improve the overall customer experience and build stronger relationships with your customers.

25

Irresistible Offers

You should creating offers that are too good to pass up, with clear benefits and a sense of urgency. You have to tailor them to your target audience's needs and wants, and limit them in time or quantity.

26

Up-, Cross- & Down-Sales

These are techniques you can be use to increase the average order value and improve your customer satisfaction.

27

Marketing Plan One-Pager

Creating a markeing plan on a single page will give you a quick guidance what to do and when.

28

Execute - Measure - Learn

Do what you planned, measure the results and learn from the analysis is the close-loop circle to drive success.

Stage 5 - Growth Hacking

29

Viral Loop

A marketing strategy that encourages your users to share a product or service with their friends, which in turn creates more users and further shares.

30

Growth Engines

Strategies and tactics that you can use to acquire and retain customers, and ultimately grow your business.

31

Profit Hacking

Profit hacking is the continuous process of improvement. By constantly looking for ways to increase profits, you can grow your business and achieve your financial goals.

32

Marginal User Magnets

A feature or benefit that attracts new users to your product or service, even if it does not provide a significant additional value to existing users.
33

Marketing Material for Viral Growth

Viral marketing material is creative and shareable content that is designed to spread quickly and widely online.

34

Partnerships

Partnerships can be a powerful way to achieve goals that would be difficult or impossible to achieve alone.

35

Marketing Automation

You can improve your marketing efficiency and effectiveness through marketing automation.
